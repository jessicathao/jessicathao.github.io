Welcome to My Academic Journey

I am a Ph.D. student in Linguistics at National Taiwan University, specializing in sociolinguistics, multilingualism, and computational linguistics. My research focuses on Vietnamese immigrant children's translanguaging practices, AI-assisted language learning, and multimodal metaphor analysis.

About Me

I am a dedicated linguistics researcher currently pursuing my Ph.D. at the Graduate Institute of Linguistics at National Taiwan University. My academic journey has taken me from Vietnam to Taiwan, where I have developed expertise in multiple areas of linguistics and applied research.

My research focuses on understanding multilingual communities, particularly Vietnamese immigrant families in Taiwan, and exploring how artificial intelligence can enhance language learning experiences.

Research Interests

My research spans multiple interconnected areas of linguistics, with a particular focus on multilingual communities and the intersection of technology with language learning.

Studying language in social contexts and communities

Multilingualism

Exploring how multiple languages interact and coexist

Computational Linguistics

Analyzing metaphors across different communication modes

Corpus Linguistics

Current Research Projects

My current work focuses on understanding how Vietnamese immigrant families navigate multilingual environments in Taiwan, and how AI technologies can be better integrated into language learning contexts.
