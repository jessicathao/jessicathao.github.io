About Me

Get to know my journey, research, and passion for teaching

Hello! I'm Le Thi Thach Thao (Jessica)

I'm a Ph.D. student in Linguistics at National Taiwan University (NTU), specializing in sociolinguistics, multilingualism, and AI literacy. My journey in academia has been driven by a deep passion for understanding how language shapes and reflects our diverse world.

My Academic Journey

I hold a Master's degree in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (TESOL) from National Kaohsiung University of Science and Technology, where I graduated with a perfect 4.0 GPA. My academic achievements include:

  • 7+ International Conference Presentations at prestigious venues including TESOL International, JALT (Japan Association for Language Teaching), and Linguapax Asia
  • 3 Published Works in academic proceedings and journals
  • Teaching Experience with over 110 students across various age groups and proficiency levels
  • 5+ Years in Taiwan as an international student and researcher

My Research Interests

My research focuses on several interconnected areas:

  • AI in Language Education: Exploring how artificial intelligence can enhance English language learning and teaching
  • Multilingualism & Sociolinguistics: Understanding language use in diverse social contexts
  • Academic Writing: Helping students develop their scholarly writing skills
  • Teacher Education: Supporting English language teachers in their professional development

Why I Started This Blog

Throughout my PhD journey, I've learned countless lessons about navigating academia, improving English proficiency, and adapting to life in a new country. I created this blog to:

  • Share practical tips and strategies for PhD students and researchers
  • Help non-native English speakers improve their language skills
  • Provide insights into studying and living in Taiwan
  • Build a community of learners and academics
