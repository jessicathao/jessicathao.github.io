Hi, I'm Thao!

Le Thi Thach Thao (Jessica) - A Vietnamese researcher, English educator, and PhD student passionate about linguistics, multilingualism, and the intersection of AI and language education.

My Journey

Originally from Vietnam, I began my academic journey at Dalat University, where I earned my B.A. in English Language Studies. My curiosity about language and education led me to Taiwan, where I've been living and studying since 2019.

I completed my Master's in TESOL and Applied Linguistics at National Kaohsiung University of Science and Technology (NKUST) with a perfect 4.0 GPA, ranking as an Excellence student. My thesis explored "The Roles of Multilingualism and Sociocultural Contexts in Shaping the Literacy Development of Children of Vietnamese Immigrant Families in Taiwan."

Currently, I'm pursuing my Ph.D. in Linguistics at National Taiwan University (NTU), where I research sociolinguistics, multilingualism, computational linguistics, AI literacy, and multimodal metaphor analysis under the guidance of Professor Chia-Rung Lu.

Research Interests

  • Sociolinguistics & Multilingualism: Understanding how language shapes identity and community, especially among immigrant families
  • AI & Language Education: Exploring how AI tools like ChatGPT and LLMs can transform English learning
  • Computational Linguistics: Using Python and corpus tools for linguistic analysis
  • Multimodal Metaphor Analysis: Analyzing visual metaphors in climate change advertising
